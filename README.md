# The Grandpa Louis H. Henzi Historical Timeline Project

Welcome to the digital archive and interactive timeline celebrating the life, family, and enduring legacy of **Louis H. Henzi (1934–2026)**. 

Built using open-source **TimelineJS** technology, this living document weaves together over 150 years of multi-generational Henzi family history alongside the rich backdrop of Cincinnati and global events that shaped the world Grandpa lived in. From the family's 19th-century roots in Germany and Switzerland to the modern generation of great-grandchildren, this project stands as a permanent tribute to his memory.

---

## 🗺️ How the Timeline is Structured

To tell a truly complete story, the archive intentionally balances two types of history:
*   **The Micro-History (Family Milestones):** Births, marriages, neighborhoods (Camp Washington, Race Road in Bridgetown), houses, childhood fires, fishing trips, and deep career milestones as a Cincinnati chemist (The Drackett Company, Fernald/National Lead consulting).
*   **The Macro-History (The World He Lived In):** Major local and national touchstones that the family witnessed or talked about around the dinner table—including the Great 1937 Flood, the breakthrough of Benadryl at UC, "Sabin Sunday" polio vaccines, the 1974 Sayler Park tornado, the Blizzard of '78, and the highs and lows of Cincinnati sports icons like Pete Rose.

## 🛠️ Technical Details & Backups

This project is rendered using **TimelineJS** by Knight Lab. The master data is securely housed in a cloud-hosted spreadsheet containing strictly structured columns (`Year`, `Month`, `Day`, `Headline`, `Text`, `Media`, etc.) that the timeline engine reads in real time.

### Backup Strategy
To prevent accidental data loss or corruption, the project data is backed up using a three-tier system:
1.  **Version History:** Automated, keystroke-by-keystroke tracking natively maintained within the cloud sheet, allowing immediate restoration to any previous state.
2.  **Local Archive:** Monthly hard-copy text exports (.csv and .xlsx format) stored locally on physical hard drives.
3.  **Sandbox Duplicate:** An isolated, internal duplicate sheet used for staging and editing new data before pushing it live to the public-facing timeline wrapper.
